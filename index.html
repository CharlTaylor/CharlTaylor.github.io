Homework 7

Festival of Economics

This week I attended Quinn and Turners talk on their book: Boom and Bust. One principle at the epicenter of their work is that a bubble requires 3 elements. Firstly, low interest rates - an incentive for exploring more lucrative asset options. Secondly, the amount of credit available to leverage more risky options. Lastly, and the component we will look at, speculative behaviour present in the economy. We would expect greater consumer confidence (CCI) over future expectations in times before a bubble burst.

The consumer confidence index maintained a position above zero for a decade before the 2008 financial crisis. This supports Turner and Quinns notion that overexuberance is an important factor when prediciting bubbles.

Marketability for houses appear to be high in years prior to the 2008 financial crash, indicated by a greater number of home sales.
